Privacy Policy

Effective: January 1, 2026 · Last updated: January 1, 2026

Grumbl ("we," "us," "the app") is a meal-memory app for iOS. We built this app to be private by design. This Privacy Policy explains what information we collect, how we use it, and the choices you have. If you do not agree with this policy, please do not use the app.

1. Who We Are

Grumbl is developed and operated by an independent developer ("the developer"). You can reach us at austinshin219@gmail.com.

2. Information We Do Not Collect

We do not collect, transmit, or store on our servers:

  • Your photos of meals
  • Your notes about meals
  • Your meal dates, labels, or any history
  • Your name, email, phone number, or contacts
  • Your location
  • Your health, fitness, or nutritional data

All meal data you create in Grumbl is stored locally on your device (and, if you choose to enable it, in your personal iCloud — see Section 4). The developer never has access to this data.

3. Information Apple Handles for Us

When you subscribe to Grumbl Pro, the purchase is processed by Apple through the App Store and StoreKit. Apple shares with us only what's needed to grant you the subscription benefit (an anonymized transaction identifier and entitlement status). We do not receive your name, email, payment method, or billing address. For more, see Apple's Privacy Policy.

4. iCloud Sync (Optional)

If iCloud sync is enabled (now or in a future version), Grumbl uses Apple's CloudKit framework to sync your meals across the devices signed in with your Apple ID. The data is stored in your personal iCloud private database — Apple's servers, not ours. The developer cannot read this data. You can disable sync at any time in iOS Settings → Apple ID → iCloud.

5. Advertising (Google AdMob)

If you have not subscribed to Grumbl Pro, the app shows advertisements supplied by Google AdMob. To select relevant ads, Google may collect:

  • Your device's Advertising Identifier (IDFA), only if you grant permission via Apple's App Tracking Transparency prompt
  • Device model, OS version, language, coarse location (country-level), and limited app-interaction data
  • Ad impressions and clicks for billing and reporting

If you decline the App Tracking Transparency prompt — or do not respond — Google receives a zeroed-out identifier and shows you non-personalized contextual ads instead. Subscribing to Grumbl Pro removes all ads entirely.

For more, see How Google uses information from its partners and the AdMob privacy notice.

6. Device Permissions

The app requests these permissions only when you use the corresponding feature:

  • Camera — to take photos of meals you log. Photos go directly into Grumbl's local storage on your device.
  • Photo Library — when you choose "Library" to import a photo, iOS shows you a picker. Grumbl receives only the photo(s) you select.
  • App Tracking Transparency — Apple's required prompt, used solely to determine whether AdMob may use your IDFA for personalized ads.

7. Children

Grumbl is rated 4+ on the App Store but is not directed at children under 13. We do not knowingly collect personal information from children. If you believe a child has provided us with information, contact us and we will delete it.

8. Your Rights

Because virtually no data is sent to us, you have direct control:

  • Delete your data — uninstalling Grumbl permanently removes all on-device data. If iCloud sync was enabled, you can also delete the data from iCloud via Settings → Apple ID → iCloud → Manage Storage → Grumbl.
  • Subscription — manage or cancel anytime via iOS Settings → Apple ID → Subscriptions.
  • Advertising — change your App Tracking Transparency choice in iOS Settings → Privacy & Security → Tracking → Grumbl, or limit ad tracking system-wide via iOS Settings → Privacy & Security → Apple Advertising.

If you live in the European Economic Area, the United Kingdom, or California, you also have the right to request information about, correction of, or deletion of any data we hold about you. Because we don't hold any, your request will typically result in a confirmation that no data exists. Contact us at the email above.

9. California Privacy Notice (CCPA)

We do not sell or share personal information for cross-context behavioral advertising as defined under the California Consumer Privacy Act. The IDFA passed to Google AdMob, when permitted, is governed by Google's own CCPA disclosures linked in Section 5.

10. Changes to This Policy

We may update this Privacy Policy from time to time. The "Last updated" date at the top will reflect the most recent revision. Material changes will be highlighted in the app or via the App Store listing.

11. Contact
